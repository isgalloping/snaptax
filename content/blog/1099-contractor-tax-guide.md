# 1099 Contractor Tax Guide

**Category:** Tax Tips
**Published:** 2026-06-20
**Description:** A practical overview of self-employment tax, quarterly payments, and Schedule C for US independent contractors.

Working as a 1099 contractor means you are your own payroll department. This guide covers the basics — not tax advice; confirm details with a CPA.

## Self-employment tax

You pay both the employee and employer portions of Social Security and Medicare on net self-employment income. Budget for this on top of income tax.

## Quarterly estimated payments

The IRS expects estimated payments four times a year if you will owe enough tax. Missing deadlines can trigger penalties. Track income and expenses monthly so you are not surprised in April.

## Schedule C

Most contractors report business income and expenses on Schedule C. Categories align with lines on that form — advertising, supplies, travel, and more. SnapTax maps receipts to Schedule C–friendly categories.

## Record keeping

Keep receipts for at least three years (longer in some cases). Digital photos stored in SnapTax count as records when they clearly show date, amount, and vendor.
